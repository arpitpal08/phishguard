General Prevention Tips

  • Check the URL before clicking or entering information
  • Look for HTTPS and the padlock icon in your browser
  • Be wary of emails asking for personal information
  • Don't click on suspicious links or download unexpected attachments
  • Use a password manager to avoid entering credentials on fake sites
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all important accounts

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Urgent requests asking for immediate action
  • Poor grammar and spelling errors
  • Generic greetings (e.g., "Dear Customer")
  • Suspicious or unusual sender email addresses
  • Requests for personal information or credentials
  • Offers that seem too good to be true

Phishing Prevention Tools

Password Managers

Password managers automatically fill credentials only on legitimate sites, helping you avoid entering information on phishing sites.

Examples: LastPass, 1Password, Bitwarden, KeePass

Two-Factor Authentication

2FA adds a second layer of security, making it harder for attackers to access your accounts even if they have your password.

Examples: Authenticator apps, SMS codes, security keys

Anti-Phishing Browser Extensions

These extensions can block known phishing sites and warn you about suspicious pages.

Examples: Web of Trust, Netcraft Extension, PhishDetector

Email Security Services

Advanced email filtering to catch phishing attempts before they reach your inbox.

Examples: Microsoft Defender, Google Workspace security
